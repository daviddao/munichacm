Meet our members


David Dao, Chair

David is a TUM M.Sc. Student and an affiliated researcher at the Broad Institute of MIT and Harvard. In his previous life, he worked on particle physics at CERN.


Raphael Hoheisel, Co-Chair

Raphi studies Engineering Science at TUM. He is interested in deep learning and computer vision and loves to organize great events.


Eileen Zhang, Treasurer

Eileen has a B.Sc. in Business Administration with focus on management science and supply chain management, a B.Sc. in Electrical Engineering and is currently doing a Master of Science. Besides her love for travel (she studied in UK, Australia and Munich), she is deeply interested in future technologies (especially autonomous driving).


Diana Papyan

Diana is a M.Sc. Student at TUM and one of the Co-Founders of the WARR Hyperloop Team. She is working on Navigation Systems for Hyperloop and is currently among the best teams in the world. Her work was featured in The Verge and others.


Homa Rasouli

Homa is a M.Sc. Student in Informatics. Originally from Iran, the recent developments have stirred her interest in the refugee and migrant crisis. As a consequence, she helped organized the first ever refugee hackathon in Munich: Hack'n'Help. Currently, she is working on semi-supervised learning in deep generative models. Besides that, she is an amazing cook.


Dmitrii Nechaev

Dmitrii is a M.Sc. Student in Inrfomatics at TUM. Having worked at a retail software start up with huge torrents of data has ignited his passion for harvesting knowledge hidden in data. In his spare time, he is predicting, who will die next in Game of Thrones. This work was featured in The Guardian, Times Magazine, BBC and many others.


Caner Hazırbaş

Caner is a PhD student at the Computer Vision Group, TUM. He is working on deep learning for computer vision applications in the fields of semantic segmentation, object recognition/classification, optical flow and depth from focus. He is a deep visioner and loves deep discussions in philosophy, history (ancient civilizations are his favorite) and technology.


Frederik Diehl

Frederik has a M.Sc. Student from TUM, doing his thesis on tactile exploration using deep learning. He holds a Bachelor of Science from Karlsruhe, is passionate about machine learning and loves to hack everything in python.


Daffiny Lin

Daffiny is a M.Sc. Student at TUM. Before that, she studied Computer Science in Taiwan. Daffiny likes to work on interdisciplinary projects at the interface between computer vision and medical applications.
